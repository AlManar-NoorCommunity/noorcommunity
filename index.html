About us

A community rooted in Palestinian priorities

Noor Community is a community rooted in Palestinian priorities and needs that brings together Palestinians and people from around the world to provide English language classes and mentoring to Palestinian adults.

Our values

Informed by context and need

We aim to support students in ways that are informed by their context and need. To achieve this, we regularly seek feedback and ideas from all community members — students, teachers, mentors and staff — and prioritise their agency and perspective.

Our purpose

Why we do this work

The goal of Noor Community is to support Palestinian young adults in:

  • pursuing their academic, professional and personal aspirations through English language learning and targeted mentoring
  • building the confidence and skills to communicate with the world, share their stories and experiences as Palestinians and contribute to a future of dignity and justice
What we offer

Support for our community

We offer the following support to our community of Palestinian young adults:

English language classes

Small-group classes focused on the learning needs and goals of the individual student, which may include proficiency exam preparation, academic English and skills, English for work or general English.

Mentoring

One-to-one support for university and scholarship applications, academic planning, job hunting and professional skills, and other life skills.

Conversation practice

Low-stakes, relaxed opportunities to practise speaking in English, discussing topics of common interest, and to meet fellow students and mentees.

Workshops

Occasional sessions or series of sessions led by experts on topics and skills of interest.

Our students

Who we work with

Our community is made up of Palestinian adults based in Gaza, the West Bank and outside Palestine.

In order to maintain quality, security and sustainability, we do not have an open student recruitment policy. New students join through referrals from existing long-term community members — students or staff — or through our partner institution, Al Manar Society.

Partners

Al Manar Society

We work in association with Al Manar Society, a Bethlehem-based organisation founded on principles of empowerment and self-determination, which supports Palestinians through comprehensive educational, cultural, and social programmes.
